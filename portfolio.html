DataAcademic Research

Amazon Go — Consumer Perception of Unmanned Retail

Explored how emerging retail technologies influence consumer behaviour, using Amazon Go as a case study to understand adoption drivers and barriers in Taiwan. Led survey design and data analysis to examine user trust, acceptance, and purchase intention, combining quantitative (n=300) and qualitative insights (n=8).

Primary responsibility： survey design & data analysis

Research Design

Quantitative

Distributed 300 surveys to analyse Taiwanese consumers' acceptance, trust, and purchase intention toward unmanned retail

Qualitative

Interviewed 8 actual Amazon Go users to understand usage experiences and latent concerns across different demographics

What might make you hesitant to try Amazon Go in person?

Respondents' main concerns centred on personal data & privacy (63.4%), followed by billing errors (45.1%) and sensor interference (40.2%), suggesting technology trust remains the key barrier to adoption.

78% of respondents saw strong potential in unmanned retail. However, adoption remains constrained by concerns around system reliability and trust particularly among older and less tech-familiar users, highlighting the need to balance technological advancement with human-centred design.

DataAcademic Research

Analysis of How Different Social Platforms Shape Different Types of Anxietyy

This is a four-person group study examining the relationship between social media usage and different types of anxiety among youth aged 16–24 across Instagram, Dcard, and Xiaohongshu.

Primary responsibility： Analysed the relationship between usage time and anxiety levels, and produced data visualisations to support findings.

Study Overview

441Surveys distributed
3Platforms studied
15Hypotheses tested
3Anxiety types

Research Methods

Survey Analysis

441 online surveys using a 7-point Likert scale to measure anxiety levels. Analysed the correlation between usage time and average anxiety scores across platforms.

In-depth Interviews

Conducted in-depth interviews with users who simultaneously use all three platforms. Applied grounded theory to explore the connection between platform usage and anxiety.

Instagram
Dcard
Xiaohongshu
Anxiety types: Appearance · Social · Academic

Key finding

The study set 15 hypotheses and used one-tailed tests to analyse the effects of different platforms on three anxiety types. Results showed that correlations between social media platforms and anxiety types differed significantly.

Research contribution

Revealed how social media usage time affects youth mental health, and identified how three types of anxiety manifest differently across platforms. Findings can inform intervention strategy design for healthier social media use, preventing anxiety escalation.
